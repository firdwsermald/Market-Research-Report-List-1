<p><p>A medical gastrointestinal stent is a tubular device inserted into the gastrointestinal tract to provide support and maintain the patency of a narrowed or obstructed segment. These stents are commonly used in conditions like esophageal cancer, bowel obstructions, and strictures to facilitate the passage of food, fluids, and waste. Made from materials such as metal or biodegradable polymers, these stents can be temporary or permanent, depending on the patient's condition.</p><p>The medical gastrointestinal stent market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.3% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by the rising prevalence of gastrointestinal diseases, an aging population, and advancements in minimally invasive surgical techniques. Additionally, increased awareness about the benefits of stenting procedures and improvements in healthcare infrastructure contribute to market expansion. </p><p>Emerging trends include the development of biodegradable stents, which minimize complications and reduce the need for additional procedures. Innovations in material science and the use of coated stents to prevent tissue ingrowth are shaping the future of this market. Overall, the combination of technological advancements and growing demand for effective gastrointestinal interventions positions the market for sustained growth.</p></p>
