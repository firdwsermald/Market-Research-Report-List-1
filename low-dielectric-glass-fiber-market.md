<p><p>The low-dielectric glass fiber market consists of three primary types: staple fiber, glass fiber yarn, and glass fiber cloth. Staple fiber is short, discrete fibers used in various applications for reinforcement and insulation. Glass fiber yarn is continuous strands suitable for weaving or knitting, offering flexibility and strength in composite materials. Glass fiber cloth, made from woven fibers, provides a robust barrier and excellent mechanical properties, serving industries like electronics, automotive, and construction, where low dielectric properties are crucial.</p></p>
