<p><p>The degradable hydrogel market is categorized into natural and synthetic types. Natural degradable hydrogels are derived from biopolymers like gelatin, alginates, and chitosan, offering biocompatibility and biodegradability, commonly used in medical applications and drug delivery systems. In contrast, synthetic degradable hydrogels are engineered from synthetic polymers such as polyvinyl alcohol and polyethylene glycol, allowing for tailored properties and controlled degradation rates. Both types serve diverse applications in healthcare, agriculture, and environmental sectors, catering to specific performance requirements.</p></p>
